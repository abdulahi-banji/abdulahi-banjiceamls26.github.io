what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the first day of the in-person section of the SAIRI program. We started off by reviewing the slides from the virtual section of the program. We were reminded about the structure of the program, our responsibilities and expectations — it was a good way to get reoriented. After that, we took headshots with our team members, as well as individual ones. Attending this orientation session was a great way to meet fellow interns, faculty mentors, and graduate students in person, and it was so exciting. After that, we attended the Data Analysis/Analytics workshop led by Dr. Jamell. We learned a lot about data analysis, including the difference between data analysis and data analytics. We were introduced to key concepts in data analytics and learned how analytical thinking is applied in real-world research contexts. I asked an interesting question about how we get more accurate model performance or data when we impute missing values in a dataset using the mean or median. The response was really explanatory, and it was so much fun listening to his session. From 
  his walkthrough, his brief explanation of how recruiting works, the expectations, and his emphasis on always seeking knowledge and being willing to learn. After that, we went for lunch before participating in a session on AI Bias and Ethics facilitated by Dr. Valerie Riggs. We learned how to distinguish between random error and systematic bias in AI output, and we had group tasks where we prompted our LLMs and deduced from the responses the underlying question being asked. After that, we had a fun interactive competition where we were given spaghetti sticks and a marshmallow and had to build the tallest structure we could without cutting the marshmallow. I participated in this activity and it was pretty fun seeing us fail and keep trying. It was great getting to meet my teammates in person and getting to converse with them more. The final activity of the day was the research lab session, where we visited our research labs. After getting in, I saw that it was a really nice building and room, and I am excited to work there. I installed the dataset my team and I will be working on and tried to set it up in PyCharm, but ran into some issues. I realized I needed to use a virtual environment and change a few settings in PyCharm to get it working. After that, I had a chat with one of my teammates while walking back to our dorms — it was an interesting conversation revolving around our project and getting to know each other better. I look forward to working in the lab tomorrow and kicking off the in-person phase of the research program.
