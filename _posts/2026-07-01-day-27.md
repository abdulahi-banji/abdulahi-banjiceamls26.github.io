what_i_learned: |
  The day started with my team and I planning which model to train and focus on, based on the results we presented at the mid-summer symposium. We decided to train the best performing model on one computer and all the P2 models on the other computer.

  After that, we wrote the code to train them before moving on to learn more about ROS (Robot Operating System), our robotic arm, and things related to the EdgeAI computer. While the rest of my teammates used Kahoot to learn about that field, my grad mentor had me debug the code for the robot arm so it could properly launch. After running the code provided, there were errors, and I had to read and understand the whole codebase, look through documentation, and work closely with the console output to figure out the problem. I realized the interpreter in PyCharm wasn't configured to see the installed library, and that I needed to install the dependencies directly in the terminal using bash commands before the packages would be recognized.

  The issue was with the Interbotix/Trossen Robotics arm package. It was showing a "module not found" error because we hadn't sourced the ROS workspace in that terminal/session. Once I sourced the workspace and launched PyCharm from that same terminal, PyCharm inherited the correct shell environment and could see the package. After that, I launched the robot arm, played around with parameters, and got everything set up. It was fun working through the documentation and troubleshooting the problem.

  There wasn't enough time before lunch to explain my findings to the team, so I watched them finish the last questions of their Kahoot session before we stepped out for lunch.

  After we came back, I explained to my teammates what was wrong, why it wasn't working, and walked them through the whole process, trying to keep it detailed but less technical. I showed them the terminal history, explained why we had to launch PyCharm from the terminal, and pointed out the small variable that controls whether or not we simulate the robot arm. After explaining, I cleared all the tabs and had each person go through the whole process themselves. I then played another Kahoot session with the undergraduates and learned more about ROS, the robot arm, and our project domain. After that, we went through our overleaf document since it had been updated from Microsoft Word, and I made a few changes to my section of the paper.

  I look forward to seeing the results and progress of the training tomorrow and to working more with the robot arm.
