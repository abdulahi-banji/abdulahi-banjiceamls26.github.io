After going through this process, I then went ahead to read another paper to better understand literature writing and further deepen my understanding of YOLO11. The paper I read today was about a study that developed a lightweight AI model called Pepper-YOLO to detect green peppers and locate their picking points for use in harvesting robots. The main challenge, according to the paper, was that green peppers blend in with leaves and often overlap, making detection difficult. The model was built on an improved version of YOLOv8n-Pose and featured several technical enhancements to boost accuracy while keeping the model small enough for low-cost devices. Tested on real vineyard images, it achieved 82.2% detection accuracy and 88.1% picking point accuracy, outperforming all compared models while being the smallest and fastest among them. I then filled out the collaborative document my team and I have, which covers objectives, methodology, dataset types, results, and limitations.
