what_i_learned: |
  The day started with us looking at the results from the model training we did yesterday. I came in excited to compare the results, only to find out that the computer had crashed. This was unexpected since we were only training one model, so we figured it was likely due to the image size and other new parameter changes we added based on the recommendation from our faculty mentor. After that, Joaquin and I used our computer to train YOLOv8-p2 m, but reduced the image size. We also plan to reduce the scale in our next model training.

  After that, my mentor led a long discussion where we talked about the scaling and size of our Lego pieces, both from the training and deployment sides. He explained everything from the transformation, how object reference works, and why we are using the QR code, then went into more detail about scaling and size, pointing out how to calculate and ensure they were all in the same range. After that, I read more about what he described before my team and I played several rounds of Kahoot to strengthen our knowledge, which turned out to be a really informative session since we learned so much in such a short duration. I look forward to testing the robot arm camera with the new sizes of QR code we have tomorrow to see which size is easiest to detect, training our model using a scale size of 0, and completing the flowchart using Canvas.
