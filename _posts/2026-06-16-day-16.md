what_i_learned: |
  I initially decided to get to the lab around 8am to do some personal study and coding, but the doors weren't open, so I remained in my dorm till around 9am. Today started off with me fixing the problem I had working with our dataset on my personal computer. I realized that all I had to do was set up a virtual environment with the required tools and make my kernel use that environment. After fixing this, I loaded my dataset, converted the .xml files to .txt files in the labels, and read and understood the code since it wasn't too long.

  My faculty mentor then showed up with our kits for the summer. I was so excited to get to work with what he had. We had an Asus Ascent GX10 AI supercomputer with an Nvidia GB10 superchip and 128GB of RAM. I then found out it costs $4,000, and I was super excited to work with it, as well as the monitor, keyboard, and other setup we had. I have to get used to that keyboard because I touch type, and I had to look at the keyboard word for word to type, which I had not done in a long time. I figured we needed something this powerful to be able to train our models without any lag. After that, we worked in 2 groups, where Joaquin and I used one of the setups and Kayla and Tolu used the other. We trained our model using 100 epochs, which took around 90 minutes. After reading and evaluating the results like the confusion matrix, the results.csv file, and other outputs and graphs, we documented key results and ran 200 epochs with a few changes in variables like the HSV value, just to compare the results with the previous training and evaluate the model's performance.

  While this was loading, my team members and I took a short break playing Skribbl while the model was training. After that, our mentor explained what was going on in the code to us, we asked further clarifying questions, and left it running. We started working on our blog post and moved a bit forward in our literature review process after being reminded by our wonderful coordinators. It was a really fun day in general. I learned a lot from the errors I encountered, the process involved in training the model, and I also got to talk, connect, and engage deeply with my team members and they are all really cool people. I look forward to seeing the results from our new training parameters, working with our new setup, and seeing what we are going to achieve tomorrow.
