what_i_learned: |
  The day started off with us gathering at the business building around 8am this morning, excited to get to the conference. The coordinators checked our IDs before entering the bus, and we started going as soon as we were all boarded. It was a pretty long journey on the road, but we were able to arrive at the conference on time.

  We got our badges, and I started to follow a graduate mentor, Farouk, as well as 3 other undergraduates. We went to a series of companies to gain insight on what they do and got merch from them. Companies like Datadog, Snowflake, Redhat, Atlassian, Accenture and MongoDB. It was a fun experience getting to connect and talk with some of the engineers in these fields and ask them questions relating to what they are doing in their companies, how they're finding it there, and other questions that would help me better understand the industry world. After that, I attended one keynote by AWS that talked about Databases and Agentic AI. Some of the terms and concepts used were industry related already, so I tried my best to keep up. We went for lunch before I attended another lab session where I built a Conversational AI bot that fronts the self-service experience. This project was interesting because it related to solutions architecture, and I learned a lot building it. After that, I took a couple of pictures around and connected a bit with people before taking the group picture and arriving back at the bus around 5:10pm.

  It was my first time attending a tech conference, and I expected it to be more overwhelming than this, but it wasn't.
